# Melodroid

A program for music research from first principles — exploring music as the meeting point
between the physics of sound and the biology of human hearing. 
Melodroid is mainly a CLI program producing various plots and printouts for study, and this companion website works as docs and explains the theory.

## Harmonic Pattern Theory

We study musical harmony as **pattern recognition of simultaneous waves**. For example, a major
chord can be expressed as fractions $\{1,\ 5/4,\ 3/2\}$. The pattern for this set of frequencies
loops every four iterations of the base wavelength, extending over four periods of time.

As can be seen, this creates a pattern of waves over time. The **wave pattern length** (WPL) is the least common multiple (LCM) of the set's denominators —
for a chord $\{p_1/q_1,\ \ldots,\ p_n/q_n\}$ the WPL is:

$$
\mathrm{WPL} = \operatorname{lcm}(q_1,\ q_2,\ \ldots,\ q_n).
$$

The idea is that the brain might have evolved to find these kinds of patterns in sound and uses them for our perception of music, especially for harmony.

### From pattern length to a hearing constraint

Given the lower frequency threshold of human hearing at about $20\ \mathrm{Hz}$, a wave pattern lasting more than roughly $50\ \mathrm{ms}$ is unlikely to be recognised as a pattern.
For melody, the [lower limit of melodic pitch](related-research/wave-pattern-length.mdx#lower-limit-of-melodic-pitch) is closer to 30hz, allowing the wave pattern only some 33ms. 

The **wave pattern duration** (WPD) for a fundamental $f_0$ is

$$
\mathrm{WPD} = \mathrm{WPL} \cdot \frac{1000}{f_0}\ \mathrm{ms}.
$$

For a fundamental of $100\ \mathrm{Hz}$, the major chord (WPL $4$) gives
$\mathrm{WPD} = 4 \cdot \frac{1000}{100} = 40\ \mathrm{ms}$.

Music (at least orchestra and opera) lives mostly in the C2–C6 range (≈ 65–1047 Hz fundamental). Lower frequencies produce longer-duration patterns, so
$100\ \mathrm{Hz}$ serves as a useful worst case for WPD. 
We will show in the theory section how this simple constraint could lead to consequences for everything from chord progressions to an optimal number of keys for a piano.

Of note is that the pattern matching idea is a form of autocorrelation. 
Another idea entirely is [neural resonance](related-research/wave-pattern-length.mdx#neural-resonance), where instead of recognising a pattern, neural oscillators in the brain mode-lock to small integer ratios.
In such a case the neuronal stability of certain integer ratios is more important than duration as a wave pattern is effectively perceived in parallell.
