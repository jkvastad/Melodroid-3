# Melody
Melody, or a tune, is a sequence of notes, usually easily recognizable.

## Octave equivalence and octave scrambling

Originally it was Deutsch[^deutsch] who took a familiar tune — the opening of _Yankee Doodle_ — and left every
note's pitch class intact while randomly reassigning each note to one of three octaves.
Listeners who were not told the tune's name failed to recognise it. The melody had dissolved, even though, by
the logic of octave equivalence, "the same notes" were being played.

## Speech and Melody degradation
In "Distinct sensitivity to spectrotemporal modulation supports brain asymmetry for speech and melody"[^albouy] 
the authors experimented with degrading either temporal or spectral components of sung acapella songs to investigate recognition of speech and melody.
The songs were degraded using the the spectrotemporal modulation framework[^elliott]:
the spectrogram is transformed into a modulation power spectrum with separate temporal-modulation (Hz) and spectral-modulation (cycles/kHz) axes. 
* Temporal degradation low-pass-filters the temporal axis, smearing the signal in time and erasing the sharp envelope changes that distinguish syllables and phonemes. 
* Spectral degradation low-pass-filters the spectral axis, blurring the fine harmonic/pitch detail across frequency. 
Temporal loss impaired speech recognition only, spectral loss impaired melody recognition only.

You can try the demo at the labs website: [https://www.zlab.mcgill.ca/spectro_temporal_modulations/](https://www.zlab.mcgill.ca/spectro_temporal_modulations/).
