# Playing Fractions

To play music we need an instrument. At one extreme, instruments such as the violin and the
human voice produce arbitrary frequencies, allowing full expression of sound. This expression comes at the cost of high
complexity since the choices are limited only by frequency resolution.

If frequencies are clustered to good fractions then perfect expression is not necessary for perfect harmony and
perhaps not even desirable. If only specific frequency relations matter, then adding meaningless
frequencies to an instrument is wasteful — an argument for **keyed** instruments, which expose a
useful subset of all frequencies and remove unnecessary complexity.

As a counterpoint, a great work sometimes needs contrast. Dissonance itself may be desirable,
much as chili — a toxin — is indisputably popular in cuisine. A naive model that labels every
toxin undesirable would miss this, just as a model labeling every non-harmonious ratio undesirable
would.

Which frequency relations matter, and ultimately which ratios does an instrument need? The
[keyboard chapter](../keyboard/a-good-keyboard.mdx) builds one up from these requirements.
