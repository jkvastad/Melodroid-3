# On a Sour Note

:::caution Deferred / speculative
This section collects open research notes on *wrong* notes — how and when a note sounds false. It
is exploratory and may change.
:::

The open question: what mechanisms make a note sound wrong?

- **Rhythm-dependent.** An expectation builds within a rhythmic block, and there seem to be rules
  for when it can be bent or broken — LCM factor overlaps, superset/subset relations,
  uniform-chord transitions — possibly spawning rhythmic sub-blocks to license harmonic changes,
  perhaps using the harmonic change itself as a rhythmic boundary.
- **Exotic effects.** LCM 15 has a flavour similar to uniform chords, better upheld by the
  harmonic minor (close to a major scale with an augmented major chord).

## Two flavours: LCM 24 vs LCM 15

Playing melody by the chord-melody tables makes two perceptual flavours apparent. LCM 24 is
light, happy, relaxed; LCM 15 is dramatic, tense, stressful — and close to the sound of uniform
chords like `0 4 8` and `0 3 6 9`.

Notes of interest:

- from `5 9 0`, the `15@0` flavour breaks on key 8 (melody `10 9 8`), a bluesy sound that pairs
  well with `24@8` (melody `8 7`).
- playing 8 from `10 1 5` breaks no flavour, as expected — key 8 matches every placement of
  `10 1 5`.
- playing 4 from `10 1 5` sounds good and full of motion, interesting because melody column 4 has
  no entry.

The brain seems to lock into a flavour once it picks up cues for parsing the sound as LCM 24 or
15, and even which placement is active. Playing chord `5 9 0` and opening with a measure of melody
`7 5 4 5` gives a clear `24@5` flavour; following it with…

- …key 11 sounds false even though it fits another LCM 24 (`24@0`) — perhaps because `5 9 0` has a
  full match `4@5`, making `24@5` the natural fit.
- …key 3 gives the LCM 15 flavour; `5 9 0` has a full match `3@0`.

:::note Loose threads
- **Preference.** Perhaps the brain prefers the LCM 24 hierarchy, so `20@0` is heard as `8@8` with
  a bad key 4.
- **Uniform chords.** Aug and dim7 have 3 vs 4 ambiguous interpretations (symmetric on the octave)
  and a distinct sound. In 12-TET the brain may essentially parse everything as LCM 24, LCM 15, or
  uniform — and "uniform" may itself be a flavour.
- **Potential perception.** The set of supersets a chord could belong to may shape how we hear it.
:::
